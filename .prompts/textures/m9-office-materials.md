# M9 Procedural Texture Prompt Pack - Office Materials

These prompts are AI-intended source prompts for future replacement assets. The current M9 batch uses deterministic local procedural generation only; no external AI image API or paid tool was used.

## Shared Style

Low-poly/stylized corporate-surreal office material set for a browser Babylon.js game. Clean fluorescent office mood, slightly wrong but readable, dry bureaucratic weirdness, not dark, no gore, no monsters, no realistic humans, no jump scares, no oppressive horror lighting. Textures should be replaceable, tile safely, and avoid visual noise.

## Assets

### m9-office-carpet

Muted office carpet texture, beige-green woven fibers, subtle grid compression, a few procedural paper-fiber flecks. Must stay low contrast so Incident Reports and signs remain readable.

### m9-wall-subtle-variation

Warm institutional wall texture with faint panel variation, tiny scuffs, and very subtle vertical seams. Should read as clean corporate drywall that has been audited too often.

### m9-ceiling-tile-light-panel

Acoustic ceiling tile texture with a restrained fluorescent light-panel motif. Pale cream surface, quiet tile seams, no stains, no horror decay.

### m9-elevator-checkout-panel

Stylized brushed metal panel for Complete Check-Out and elevator surfaces. Soft vertical bands, understated button marks, calm corporate tone.

### m9-incident-report-paper

Paper texture for Incident Reports and form stacks. Warm off-white form paper, pale rule lines, faint red audit stamp geometry, readable at first-person distance.

### m9-clarity-abstract-overlay

Abstract Clarity-related overlay: clean procedural linework, pale cyan and muted gold accents, slight registration mismatch. It should feel bureaucratically uncertain, not supernatural or frightening.
